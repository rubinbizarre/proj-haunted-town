rm_inside should probably not be implemented

unless each npc's x,y and state can be retrieved when switching rooms as if they were always existing
so, say we go inside a haunted building at 13:00, and then come out of the haunted building at 23:00,
you'd expect that each npc would be in their home by now or on their way home.

since npc objects cant continue their paths while the room is different, their x,y, path and state
would need to be determined at the point of returning to rm_main using only time

when going inside a haunted building, it may instead be better to effectively simulate a room change
by loading an object which:
	- has a large background sprite
	- disables any input on all objects outside the building
	- disables (or muffles?) any sound occuring outside the building
this would enable the world outside to carry on as usual

			GENERAL NOTES ON GAMEPLAY
 - hauntable world objects are not immediately accessible. they, like the buildings, are purchaseable
	for a haunt point price
 - Nev should always lock the object when shutting down a haunted world object
 - the player starts with a certain amount of Haunt Points
 - if the player is not careful, they can lose all of their Haunt Points, resulting in Game Over (can't do anything without them really)
	- maybe in a future version, the player can 'make a deal with the devil' or something to keep playing
		- this feels like it would be better served as a general gameplay modifier that players can select
		  before starting a new game, rather than during one
 - there could be some kind of more general skill tree locked via haunt points, which would allow for a
   greater sense of progression?
	- for example, the player starts out as a low-level or weak spirit that can only haunt one world object at a time
		- thus one aspect of the skill tree would be related to world objects, allowing the player to haunt
		  multiple at a time, enabling faster HPaccumulation/faster rate of growth whilst also increasing
		  game complexity with Nev being on the prowl. feels like player becomes a stronger spirit.
	- maybe lower zoom levels (seeing more of the world at once) is locked via skill tree also, as this makes Nev,
	  amongst other things, easier to track and locate
